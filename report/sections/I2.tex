\subsection{I.2 Understanding the Baseline}

\paragraph{Policies (a).}
All three share the frozen Gemma-3-1B weights and differ only in LoRA adapters. $\pi_\theta$ is the
actor (LoRA matrices in \path{scripts/model.py}, the only trainable parameters);
$\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}$ is the frozen base (zero-adapter) policy passed to \texttt{RLCluster}, the KL
anchor with no trainable parameters; $\pi_{\mathrm{old}}$ is the actor snapshot that generated the
rollouts. With \texttt{NUM\_ITERATIONS=1} Tunix skips a separate old-actor pass --- inside the loss
the old log-probabilities are the current per-token log-probabilities under \texttt{stop\_gradient}.

\paragraph{Group size and estimator (b).}
$K=2$ (\texttt{NUM\_GENERATIONS=2} in \path{scripts/config.py}). With the default
\texttt{advantage\_estimator="grpo"} Tunix uses the standard group-normalised
$\hat A_i = (r_i-\bar r_q)/(s_q+10^{-6})$, with $\bar r_q,s_q$ the mean and sample s.d.\ of the $K$
rewards for a prompt --- the ordinary group-mean baseline, not the leave-one-out (\texttt{rloo})
estimator.

\paragraph{Reward decomposition (c).}
\path{scripts/rewards.py} sums four terms.
\path{match_format_exactly}/\path{match_format_approximately} are format-shaping (they reward the
\path{<reasoning>}/\path{<answer>} tags regardless of the arithmetic); \path{check_answer} is the
true correctness reward (largest payoff for the exact GSM8K number, partial/negative otherwise);
\path{check_numbers} is a numeric fallback. With correctness alone, early on most siblings score the
same (usually zero), so the within-group $s_q\to0$ and the standardised advantages vanish; the
shaping terms make two siblings differ before the model can do the maths.

\paragraph{Clipped surrogate (d).}
Implemented as \texttt{grpo\_loss\_fn} in \path{tunix/rl/algo_core.py} (selected by
\texttt{policy\_loss\_fn="grpo"}): it forms the new/old per-token log-prob ratio $\rho$ and minimises
$\max\{-\hat A\,\rho,\;-\hat A\,\mathrm{clip}(\rho,1-\varepsilon,1+\varepsilon)\}$ (a loss, hence the
sign flip vs the lecture objective). \texttt{EPSILON=0.2} clips $\rho$ to $[0.8,1.2]$. The deviation
from the lecture form is granularity: ratios are per completion-token, aggregated with
\texttt{loss\_agg\_mode="sequence-mean-token-mean"} and the scalar group advantage broadcast over
tokens, rather than a single sequence-level ratio.
