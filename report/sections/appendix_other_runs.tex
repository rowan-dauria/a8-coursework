\subsection{Other training-parameter changes}

Beyond the group-size sweep (Table~\ref{tab:ksweep}) and the reward reweighting reported in I.3,
the team ran several further single-knob experiments to probe the baseline: the learning rate,
the LoRA rank/$\alpha$, the reference-KL coefficient $\beta$, and an explicit length penalty
plus an empty-completion penalty. All use seed 42, the default 3364-step schedule, and the same
greedy 64-example GSM8K eval protocol as the rest of the report. For reference, the frozen base
model (\(\pi_{\text{ref}}\)) scores \textbf{47.38\%} on the full 1319-example test set.

\noindent
The dominant pattern echoes the $K$ sweep: the $K{=}8$ variants hold high, stable eval reward,
but that signal does not guarantee high greedy accuracy. Raising the learning rate and doubling
the LoRA rank each score only $29.69\%$, while the length-penalty variant reaches $50.00\%$, still
below the $59.38\%$ $K{=}8$ baseline. The $K{=}2$ outcomes are more variable: a stronger
$\beta{=}0.32$ KL penalty and the length penalty finish at $0.00\%$ and $4.69\%$, respectively,
whereas reducing $\beta$ to $10^{-6}$ retains base-level performance at $51.56\%$. Extra adapter
capacity ($32.81\%$) and the empty-completion penalty ($14.06\%$) mitigate but do not prevent the
$K{=}2$ degradation. These are single-seed, 64-example results, and the hard/medium-data accuracy
remains unverified, so the experiments are recorded here rather than in the main I.3 comparison.
